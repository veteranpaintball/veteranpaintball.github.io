Game/Fields

Maps & Game Types

King of the Hill

King of the Hill in paintball is a game mode where teams or individuals compete to control a designated area called the "hill." Players start at their bases and must reach the hill to establish control. The first player to enter the hill becomes the "King" and defends it against opponents. Other players try to eliminate the King and take control for themselves. The game continues as teams battle for control, using tactics and teamwork to gain an advantage. Eliminated players must leave the game temporarily. The team or player who holds the hill the longest wins the game.

Speedball

Speedball is a fast-paced form of paintball played on a small, symmetrical field with inflatable bunkers. Two teams compete to eliminate opponents or capture a flag at the center and return it to their starting position. Players use speed, agility, and teamwork to navigate the field, take cover, and engage in rapid-fire exchanges. The game is known for its high-intensity action and quick decision-making.

Nuketown

Nuketown paintball is a game mode that recreates the iconic Nuketown map from the Call of Duty video game series in a real-life paintball setting. Players compete in a smaller-sized arena with structures and obstacles resembling the virtual environment. The objective varies but often includes capturing flags, eliminating opponents, or controlling key positions. It offers fast-paced, close-quarters combat, requiring speed, strategy, and accuracy from players.

Castle

Castle is a game in which teams compete to control and defend a castle or fortification. One team defends the castle, while the other team tries to infiltrate and take control. Defenders aim to repel attackers and maintain control, while attackers strive to eliminate defenders and capture the castle. The game is characterized by strategic positioning, teamwork, and intense battles within and around the castle.
